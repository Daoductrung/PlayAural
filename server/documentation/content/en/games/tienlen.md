\*\*Tien Len\*\*

Tien Len is a Vietnamese climbing card game about shedding your hand, controlling tempo, and choosing the right moment to spend powerful 2s, four-of-a-kind, or consecutive-pair chops. PlayAural supports both Southern Tien Len and Northern Tien Len.

\*\*Gameplay\*\*

Tien Len uses a standard 52-card deck. Each player receives 13 cards.

\* \*\*Card ranking:\*\* 3 is lowest, then 4, 5, 6, 7, 8, 9, 10, J, Q, K, A, and 2 is highest.
\* \*\*Suit ranking:\*\* Spades are lowest, then Clubs, Diamonds, and Hearts.
\* \*\*First hand:\*\* The player holding the 3 of Spades starts. The opening play must include that card. If the 3 of Spades was not dealt in a 2- or 3-player hand, the lowest dealt card starts instead.
\* \*\*Later hands:\*\* The first-place finisher from the previous hand starts the next hand.
\* \*\*On your turn:\*\* Play a legal combination that beats the current trick, or pass.
\* \*\*Locked pass:\*\* Passing locks you out of the current trick until the table clears, except for legal Southern chop windows.
\* \*\*Trick reset:\*\* When everyone who can answer has passed, the last unbeaten player clears the trick and leads a fresh one.
\* \*\*Hand places:\*\* The hand does not end when the first player goes out. That player takes first place and drops out; the remaining players continue until only one player has cards left.

In PlayAural, select one or more cards from your hand, then use \*\*Play Selected Cards\*\* to commit that combination.

\*\*Valid Combinations\*\*

\* \*\*Single:\*\* one card.
\* \*\*Pair:\*\* two cards of the same rank.
\* \*\*Triple:\*\* three cards of the same rank.
\* \*\*Four of a Kind:\*\* four cards of the same rank.
\* \*\*Straight:\*\* three or more consecutive ranks.
\* \*\*Consecutive Pairs:\*\* Southern mode only; three or more consecutive pairs.

\*\*Southern Tien Len\*\*

Southern Tien Len is the looser, more tactical variant.

\* Pairs only need the same rank.
\* Straights may mix suits.
\* 2s cannot be used in straights.
\* Three or more consecutive pairs are legal and may be used to open a fresh trick.
\* Four-of-a-kind, three consecutive pairs, four consecutive pairs, and five consecutive pairs are chopping tools.

\*\*Southern Chopping\*\*

\* A four-of-a-kind or three consecutive pairs can chop a single 2.
\* A four-of-a-kind or four consecutive pairs can chop a pair of 2s.
\* Five consecutive pairs can chop three 2s.
\* A higher chopping combination of the same shape can chop a lower one, such as higher three consecutive pairs over lower three consecutive pairs.
\* A four-of-a-kind or four consecutive pairs can chop three consecutive pairs.
\* Four consecutive pairs can chop a four-of-a-kind.
\* Legal chops may be used to jump in out of turn and may also let a player return after passing.

\*\*Southern Instant Wins\*\*

At the deal, Southern hands can win immediately with:

\* \*\*Six pairs:\*\* exactly six pair units in the 13-card hand.
\* \*\*Five consecutive pairs:\*\* five or more consecutive ranks with at least two cards of each rank, excluding 2s.
\* \*\*Three consecutive triples:\*\* three consecutive ranks with at least three cards of each rank, excluding 2s.
\* \*\*Four 2s:\*\* all four 2s.
\* \*\*Dragon straight:\*\* at least one card of every rank from 3 through Ace.
\* \*\*Six consecutive pairs:\*\* six or more consecutive ranks with at least two cards of each rank, excluding 2s.
\* \*\*Four triples:\*\* four ranks with at least three cards of each rank.
\* \*\*Two four-of-a-kind sets:\*\* two separate ranks with all four suits.
\* \*\*Same color:\*\* 12 or more cards that are all red or all black.

\*\*Northern Tien Len\*\*

Northern Tien Len is stricter.

\* Pairs must be the same rank and the same color: Spades plus Clubs, or Diamonds plus Hearts.
\* Straights must be one suit only.
\* 2s cannot be used in straights.
\* When answering a trick, your play must preserve the same suit or color structure.
\* A single 2 may be chopped by a four-of-a-kind.
\* Southern consecutive-pair chopping does not apply in Northern mode.
\* You cannot finish by playing 2s, and you cannot make a play that leaves only 2s in your hand.

\*\*Scoring\*\*

PlayAural uses virtual coins instead of real-money settlement. Each player starts with the selected number of coins. Settlements use 10-coin stake units. Players who reach 0 coins are eliminated, and the last player with coins wins the match.

For normal hands, first place receives rank payments from the other players:

\* \*\*2 players:\*\* last pays 10 coins to first.
\* \*\*3 players:\*\* 2nd pays 10 coins to first; last pays 20 coins to first.
\* \*\*4 players:\*\* 2nd pays 10 coins to first; 3rd pays 20 coins to first; last pays 30 coins to first.

Extra betting penalties also apply:

\* \*\*Chops:\*\* when a player chops 2s or a chopping combination, the chopped player pays the chopper.
\* \*\*Leftovers:\*\* at hand settlement, remaining 2s, four-of-a-kind, and Southern consecutive-pair chopping cards pay penalties to the first-place finisher.
\* \*\*Instant wins:\*\* normal rank settlement is skipped. Each opponent pays the instant winner according to the instant-win hand, from 20 coins for six pairs up to 90 coins for a dragon straight.

If a player does not have enough coins to pay a transfer, they pay as much as they can, stop at 0, and are eliminated before the next hand.

\*\*Game Options\*\*

\* \*\*Variant:\*\* Choose Southern Tien Len or Northern Tien Len (default Southern Tien Len).
\* \*\*Starting Coins:\*\* Choose each player's starting bankroll (default 50 coins, choices: 20, 50, 100, or 200 coins).
\* \*\*Turn Timer:\*\* Choose the per-turn time limit (default Unlimited, choices: 10, 15, 20, 30, 45, 60, or 90 seconds, or Unlimited).

\*\*Keyboard Shortcuts\*\*

\* \*\*Space:\*\* Play selected cards.
\* \*\*P:\*\* Pass. If your personal \*\*Confirm risky actions\*\* preference is enabled, press Pass twice to confirm.
\* \*\*C:\*\* Check the current trick.
\* \*\*H:\*\* Read your hand.
\* \*\*E:\*\* Read card counts.
\* \*\*V:\*\* Hear the selected variant.
\* \*\*Shift+T:\*\* Check the turn timer.
